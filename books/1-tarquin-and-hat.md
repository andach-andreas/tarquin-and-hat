# Tarquin and Hat

## Premise 

It centers on the adventures of Tarquin, a young wizard on a planet recovering from an unknown event approximately 800 years ago known as "The Fall". During the fall, everything was lost. It appears that the vast majority of the planet's living population was wiped out without trace, all knowledge, though buildings were left standing.

Tarquin is gay, and the prevailing authority of The Natural Path, essentially the sole significant religion on the planet, does not accept homosexuality as part of his teachings. Tarquin is recovering from losing Jeremy, the first and only man whom he loved, when they were caught together at a monastary for young wizards. Both of them were chased out of the monastary, and Tarquin has not seen Jeremy since.

Tarquin is the son of a lord, and enjoys high status. Due to his immense skill with magic, and his father's connections, the church and wider society just ignored the problem of his homosexuality, though his conservative father does not accept him.

Tarquin makes his living as a Vault Hunter, buying locations of mysterious vaults from The Natural Path, going to them, clearing them out of their dangerous metallic humanoid inhabitants (the only pre-fall things known to have survived) and finding purple gems that allow for the casting of magic. The Natural Path buys these gems back from Vault Hunters the world over. It's a dangerous job, but Tarquin is very good at it, and works along. Due to the danger, the majority of hunters work in teams. 

At the start of the book, Tarquin finds Hat, who is a magical talking Hat who has no memory. Magical items are known in this world, presumed to be leftovers from before the fall. Hat is initially mildly useful at spotting danger, and Tarquin realises quickly that they are bonded somehow. Tarquin can't remove him from his head - every time he goes to do so, he simply decides not to. 

Hat is actually Micah, a god from a different solar system, brought to Tarquin's planet by the ancient wizard Nin Mosbeam 800 years ago, and Hat comes to understand he is someone else by the second third of the book, but neither of them understand precisely how powerful he is by the end of the book. 

## Plot

Tarquin uncovers the secrets of the Natural Path and saves Jeremy from a dungeon in which he is captured.
