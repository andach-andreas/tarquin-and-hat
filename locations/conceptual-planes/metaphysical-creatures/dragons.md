# Dragons

Dragons are created by Euales, The Harmonium. They come in true and drake form. 

## Appearance and Characteristics

### True Form

In true form, they are demigods in their own right. Shapeshifters dedicated to order, collaboration and truth. 

In Pangenia, they often act as mediators or judges for two parties who mutually agree to be bound by the dragon's decision. They can - and usually do - take humanoid form msot of the time but have the ability. 

They have no colour, and can shift colours in their dragon form at will, many tending to remain an iridescent rainbow. 

They are well-known for having distinct personalities, are ancient, nigh-invulnerable, and incredibly rare and elusive, sometimes disappearing into humanoid form for millenia at a time. Generally, they favour principle of non-intervention in mortal matters, but each dragon acts as per their own intentions. 

Dragons speak Draconic, the language of Euales, and almost all have attained fluency in countless hundreds of languages across the universe. 

### Drake Form

Dragon drakes are large lizards, roughly the size of komodo dragons, who have the full ability to speak in Draconic, and many learn multiple languages too, though they lack the proficiency of their true form brothers. 

They are animalistic and simple, still needing to eat and breathe but still nigh-invulnerable. 

## Integration into Societies

Dragons do not generally integrate into mortal society, sometimes choosing to live on a planet for some time, usually a few years, but sometimes centuries or millenia. Even when they live in a place for a long time, they generally do not involve themselves significantly in society, sometimes simply enjoying the geography or the company. 

If they choose to interact with mortals, they typically act as priests or scientists, seeking to guide and nudge society to improve without taking a leadership or military role to enforce this. 

On very rare occasions, a dragon may take a champion, a mortal to whom they will ascribe increasing power. O'rel is one of those examples.
