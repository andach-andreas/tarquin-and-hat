# Khar'thesk

## Appearance and Characteristics

The khar'thesk are tiny black obsidian fragments, which float aimlessly when they wish, but are most often found simply lying on the ground. They are sharp physical objects, and can be formed into weapons or used to augment blades or armor. 

By itself, a khar'thesk does nothing. It only acts when touched with intent. An individual can pick one up - off the floor or out of the air, and clench their fist around it or cut themselves to draw blood. 

The individual's soul is then drawn to a facsimile of the plane of Krision to confess to Ortharion himself. Unknown to most, this isn't the true plane, and is merely a sort of "local copy" - and one confesses to the khar'thesk one holds. To be transported to the true plane, one has to hold a significant secret or confession, be incredibly important, or be a very powerful arcane caster. 

Nobody can lie when in this state, and nobody can lie when touching a khar'thesk. 

Their effects only happen when drawing blood, making them interesting and terrifying weapons. They can't be easily touched without cutting yourself, but by holding them in even just a simple piece of cloth they can be transported relatively safely. 

The khar'thesk are impossible to forge, but can be worked onto weapons or armor by forging the metal around the khar'thesk itself.
