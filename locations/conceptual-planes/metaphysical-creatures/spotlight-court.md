# Spotlight Court

## Appearance and Characteristics

The Spotlight Court are sentient shadows from Dramalia, The Theatre of the Fractured Will, who act out stage plays in silence. They puff into and out of existence in a cloud of harmless black smoke. 

They appear at will, and when they want to, and cannot be controlled by mortals. They are unable to harm or be harmed by any means, and show no aggressive intent except as part of their dramatic performance. 

### Grand Masques

These are the only members of the court who can speak, and act as protagonists. On rare occasions, they appear on their own, typically to inspire, but sometimes to chastise. 

They speak telephatically, and any sentient being can understand their words if they understand a language, but only to the extent that their own language can express whatever is being said. Sentient, non-intelligent beings like animals might get the emotional gist of the performance, but would not be able to understand complex philosophical concepts. 

They are able to appear at absolutely any size, even celestial, only taking on this form to warn of planetary catastrophes like an asteroid impact. 

### The Chorus

These are typical humanoid-shaped shadows that form the primary actors and cannot speak. 

### Stage Hands

These are smaller, foot-length shades that act as props for the stage. On occasion, these forms will form a sort of bond with a hero and act to warn them - cryptically - such as surrounding a door handle with their 'bodies' to show danger inside. 

For certain heroes, they can bond permanently or semi-permanently, and accept basic commands. They remain unable to speak, and the small number of bonded heroes typically name them as odd familiars, forming a symbiotic relationship with their Stage Hands. 

## Integration into Society

Harmless, societies in which the Spotlight Court is common will typically form a more individualistic and poetic society in which arts and drama are highly valued. 

The occasional and unpredictable stage plays are often viewed by mortals as a sign, and superstitions and interpretations of plays will vary wildly.
