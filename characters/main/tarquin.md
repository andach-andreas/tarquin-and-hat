# Tarquin

Gideon Tarquin  is a 23 year old man of noble birth from Hesten, a small village in the mountains.

## Childhood

When he was a boy, his parents were close to him. His father was stern and businesslike, but proud of his son. His mother was loving and kind. He was always expected to take command of the town and received a formal education.

At 11, his father often had him accompany trading trips, particularly to Leywina, as their closest trading partner. It  was here that Tarquin found a love of magic, and his parents accepted his study, though neither of them had any particular inclination for the arcane themselves.

His parents were not particularly religious, and Tarquin did not believe in the teaching of the church himself, until the age of about 16, when his parents underwent a religious conversion of sorts and became more devout. They accepted Tarquin’s atheism and moved to disapprove of his religious study, but did not outright ban it. When he was 17, in a bid to focus him away from the arcane, they asked him to spend a year at the Mount Ashton Monastary, selling this as an opportunity for Tarquin to round his education in other ways, as well as endear himself to Hesten’s small but growing religious population.

## The Monastary

Tarquin agreed, more out of respect for his parents than any other reason. He was pleasantly surprised to find that the Monastary did permit arcane study, albeit in a highly limited version and he learned little in the months he  spent there.

A few months in, he fell for a boy named Jeremy about his age, and they formed a relationship for a few months until they were caught by the Head Monk. Homosexuality being forbidden by the church, they were both arrested  but Tarquin managed to escape using his magic to help. He had hoped that Jeremy too, had escaped in the struggle and waited for him for three days under the tree where they first kissed, but gave up and returned to Hesten in disgrace.

When he returned, word had already reached Hesten about Tarquin’s excommunication. His parents, who had grown even more devout in the six months, were furious and his mother had a breakdown. They begrudglingly allowed him to stay as the church taught that family was sacred but his mother was almost catatonic and his father refused to speak to him.

## Vault Hunter

After a few days, Tarquin left for Leywina and started using “Tarquin” instead of “Gideon” to separate himself from his family. He took a job vault hunting and found himself skilled at it, and spent the next few years doing that, as well as some study with scholars. He became known as an excellent vault hunter and the church, while knowing of his homosexuality, ignored it because he bought them a lot of crystals and artifacts.

Tarquin has so far cleared 23 vaults, the most of any individual vault hunter, and Vault 433 is his 2<sup>t h</sup>4. He’s found two artifacts:

* A bag that seemed to magically carry anything.
* A waterskin that refilled overnight with fresh drinking water.

He sold both of them to the church for a more than decent amount of gold, as well as a lot of the crystals he found.
