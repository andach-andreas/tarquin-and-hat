# Universe

## The Primordials

The entire multiverse is ‘ruled’ by Void, which is an entity that seeks to balance all things. Void created Time as a wife for himself, and then together they had children, Creation, Being and Destruction.

Void and Time fell out over Destruction, their third child. The existence of Time and their children weakened Void, but Destruction would destroy everything, including Time itself, eventually returning the universe to nothingness, and only Void would exist.

Time and Creation conspired, and Creation herself birthed a child without Void’s knowledge. That child was Will, and she gave up a part of her power to living beings and trusted them to limit Destruction’s power without being bound by the same limitations as the Primordials.

The creation of Will was not in keeping with Void’s balance, and another being was created, though Time and Creation didn’t know it. This was the antithesis of Will, and was not named, but Void calls him Memory, and keeps his existence a secret from everyone else.

Destruction wanted to create a multiverse that ended – a Big Crunch, in a way. All things would be destroyed and cease to exist, leaving only void.

Will and Creation instead want to create a universe in which Destruction destroys at the rate that Creation creates, one that lasts forever, fluctuating and in which life can exist indefinitely.

## Will Created Pangaeia

Before Will existed, mutliverses were created and destroyed with life in them, but no free will. Will existing allowed for higher life as well as intelligent life to evolve and create itself without Creation’s direct influence. Will could also create beings directly, and ascribe in them a tiny piece of his power, to further his goals.

Will never created children with power levels even close to that of the Primordials, as he didn’t want to just recreate the same problem that Void and Time had with Destruction – seeing the power get away from them and  out of their control.

Will referred to the existing multiverses as either “dead” (if no life existed in them) or “blind” (where life existed, but had no free will yet). Instead of changing things inside Creation’s existing multiverses, he instead decided to start by creating one of his own, to test his own power.

He created what would later be known as Πανγένεια (Pangeneia) – which in his divine language roughly translates to “Creation of all Things”. His first universe he created was a torus shape, and he in turn created a being to oversee it, calling it “Pan”, which means ‘All’. Pan was the first of what would be called the Celestials – beings with power levels less than Primordials, but more than what Humans would eventually call “Gods”. Will created Pan with the instruction to keep balance and observed her to see how this would go – as a kind of experiment. Will deliberately decided not to task it with limiting the power of destruction, instead hoping that life would find out how to do that as a side-effect. He learned from his parents the problems that come with providing  close-ended instructions.

## Pan Created the Conceptual Planes

Pan saw the universe that had been created for it, and in turn, started to experiment, believing this was all that was, unaware of the existence of the further multiverse.

It started by creating Chaos and Order in a pair, and used the Torus to fling these out into the nothingness surrounding it. They separated and formed the first axis on which things could be measured, so Pan created a variety of living species in Pangeneia itself, which had a slight but noticeable tendency to organise on the torus consistent with the outside Chaos and Order. Those creatures who happened to prefer structure tended towards the edge of the torus on the Order side, and vice-versa.

Pan then created the concepts of Collaboration and Tension – the tendency of living beings to seek to help or hinder one another, which would later become known by humans as Good and Evil, though that wasn’t a completely fair characterisation. Again, Pan used the Torus to fling these out into the nothingness, and observed that they settled into a resonance with Chaos and Order to form powerful separate quadrants outside Pangeneia, and in turn noted that her subjects were drawn to the corresponding areas inside the Torus.

Pan created one final set of concepts, Truth/Illusion, and the corresponding stuff happened.

Pan let reality play out for a time, and saw the concepts coalesce into eight other planes, which in turn were ruled  by a Celestial formed from her own Will, representing the apex of the combination of these concepts.

## Pan Created the Material Plane

Pan let the balance of the Planes play out and allowed the Celestials to conflict with each other, deciding on the need for one final plane, created directly by itself, connected to all eight of the Conceptual Planes, but not the concepts directly.

This was the last plane to be created, as Pan wasn’t powerful enough to directly imbue anything with enough power to create further existence.

Pan imbued the material realm with the desire to, as with everything, seek balance, and decided to create a system whereby massive dead starts would support tiny, lifebearing planets. The star would in each case be the conduit for the magical fields and would impassively grant these to their planets.

## The Migration

After the creation of the Material Plane, Will's influence bled into other nearby universes and brought over sentience from these, not native to the prime universe, and many shaped by the nature of the conceptual planes. Each plane created a link to another universe, bringing over a range of multiplanar beings.
