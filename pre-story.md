# Pre-Story: The Fall of Terra and the Sealing of Destruction

## 1. The Genesis of the Threat
**Destruction's Awakening:** The Primordial Destruction, observing the multiverse created by Will, grew restless. Instead of a "Big Crunch," he saw a universe that persisted, fluctuating, and teeming with life—a direct affront to his nature. He became aware of Will's "lock": the collective free will of mortals, subtly containing his power.

**Karzairos's Corruption:** Destruction found an eager agent in **Karzairos**, the Celestial of Skepsyris (Chaos + Tension + Truth). Karzairos, driven by a desire to expose the "truth" of existence and dismantle all illusions, was susceptible to Destruction's influence. Destruction offered Karzairos a direct conduit to his power—a **red gem**—and whispered promises of a "unified" universe under Karzairos's chaotic truth.

**The Unity Coalition:** Karzairos, in turn, empowered a small number of Planars from Skepsyris, forming the "Unity Coalition." They believed they were working towards a glorious, unified dictatorship, unaware they were merely pawns in Destruction's plan to reduce all to ashes.

## 2. The War of the Planes and the Fall of Micah
**The First War (c. 950 years before present):** The Unity Coalition's influence spread rapidly, sparking a conflict that spanned multiple planes. Terra, a vibrant world and the domain of the Nature God **Micah**, became a key battleground due to its strategic location and strong magical ley lines. The fighting was brutal and conventional, involving armies of mortals, Planars, and constructs.

**Micah's Sacrifice (c. 950 years before present):** In a decisive battle on Terra, the Unity Coalition unleashed a weapon of such potency that it nearly obliterated Micah. The God, fighting on the front lines to protect his world, was mortally wounded, his physical form disintegrating. **Nin Mosbeam**, a powerful Planar wizard and a key figure in the opposing force—the **Accord of Spheres**—was present. In a desperate act to save his friend and ally, Nin performed a forbidden transference ritual, binding Micah's fading consciousness to the nearest object at hand: his own wide-brimmed hat.

**The Aftermath:** Micah survived as a sentient object, retaining his divine knowledge but stripped of his physical agency. Terra, however, was devastated. The war had depopulated the planet, leaving behind ruined cities, scarred landscapes, and a weary, diminished population. For the next century and a half, Terra remained a backwater, a scarred monument to the cost of the war.

## 3. The Discovery and the Heist
**The Accord of Spheres:** Nin Mosbeam was a high-ranking member of the **Accord of Spheres**, a loose alliance of powerful Planars, Celestials, and free-willed entities dedicated to maintaining the balance of the multiverse. The Accord discovered the Unity Coalition's ultimate plan: to use the red gem to destabilize Pangeneia and unleash Destruction.

**The Heist:** Nin, Micah (now the Hat), and an elite team of Planar specialists from the Accord infiltrated the Crucible of Known Madness (Skepsyris). They struck during a critical ritual intended to fully awaken the gem. The mission was a success, but the team suffered heavy casualties. They secured the volatile gem, but its raw power acted as a beacon, making it impossible to hide for long.

## 4. The Choice of Terra and the Sanction
**The Strategic Sacrifice:** The Accord debated where to hide the artifact. Nin and Micah proposed Terra. The planet was already war-torn and depopulated from the previous century's conflict. Its existing, ancient network of **Vaults**—built by a long-dead, advanced civilization—offered a perfect physical defense. More importantly, Micah's residual divine connection to the planet could help mask the gem's signature.

**The Hard Decision:** Both Nin and Micah knew the risks. Bringing the gem to Terra would draw the Unity Coalition's gaze. The Accord's leadership, including avatars of Order, calculated that the only way to truly secure the gem was to isolate it completely. They proposed the **Sanction**: a synchronized bombardment of Terra with raw conceptual energy from all eight planes. This would "irradiate" the planet, killing all life and severing all portal connections for centuries, buying the Accord time to regroup and preventing the Coalition from accessing the gem.

**Nin's Volunteerism:** Nin, understanding the necessity, volunteered to be the one to place the gem. He argued that he needed to be there to ensure the specific vault was sealed correctly and to personally oversee the final moments. He knew this was a suicide mission. Micah, trapped in the Hat, agreed. He saw it as a final service to the multiverse, a way to make his previous sacrifice meaningful.

## 5. The Fall (c. 800 years before present)
**The Preparation:** Nin returned to Terra. He used his remaining time to activate the ancient Vaults, ensuring their **Guardian Constructs**—humanoid robots powered by arcane crystals—were fully operational. He placed the red gem in the most secure vault, programming its guardian to be exceptionally lethal. He told the few remaining inhabitants of Terra that a "Great Cycle" was ending, urging them to store their knowledge in the other vaults, creating a planet-wide decoy system.

**The End:** The Sanction began. Portals tore open in the sky, bathing the planet in a chaotic, lethal spectrum of conceptual energy. Every living thing on the surface—people, animals, plants, bacteria—was instantly extinguished. Nin, standing before the sealed vault, was consumed by the energies, his sacrifice complete.

**The Survivor:** Micah, being an inanimate object, was immune to the biological effects of the radiation. He lay in the dust of a dead world, the only sentient thing left on the planet. The Guardian Constructs, also non-biological, remained in their vaults, their "peaceful mode" protocols lost or corrupted by the energy wave, leaving them in a permanent state of aggressive defense.

## 6. The Long Silence and the Second Genesis
**The Bleeding:** For centuries, Terra was a silent, radioactive tomb. No portals could open; no one could leave or enter. The Accord believed the threat was contained. The Unity Coalition believed the gem was lost.

**The Rebirth:** Slowly, over hundreds of years, Micah's divine nature began to leak out. His subconscious desire for life and nature acted as a terraforming force. The dead soil began to sprout new, strange flora. New life forms evolved rapidly, twisted and empowered by the lingering conceptual radiation and Micah's druidic magic. This "Second Genesis" created the monsters, magic, and races of the modern era. The ruins of the pre-Fall civilization became myths, their technology indistinguishable from magic to the new inhabitants.
